Introduction

National happiness is one of the most important things for people to see whether the country is good for living and government can make correct decision. This report gained recognition as governments, organizations and civil society increasingly use happiness indicators to inform their policy-making decisions. The purpose of choosing this task is to find out which factors are more important for people to live a happy life. Based on the results, people and countries can focus on more critical factors to achieve a higher level of happiness.
This project uses visualization to help people to understand the factor that cause the difference of national happiness index.

Dataset

The datasets include 155 countries' economy, family, health, freedom, trust, generosity, and dystopia residual score in 2015.
The datasets collected from World Happiness Report.

**What is Dystopia?
Dystopia is an imaginary country that has the world’s least-happy people. The purpose in establishing Dystopia is to have a benchmark against which all countries can be favorably compared (no country performs more poorly than Dystopia) in terms of each of the six key variables, thus allowing each sub-bar to be of positive (or zero, in six instances) width. The lowest scores observed for the six key variables, therefore, characterize Dystopia. Since life would be very unpleasant in a country with the world’s lowest incomes, lowest life expectancy, lowest generosity, most corruption, least freedom, and least social support, it is referred to as “Dystopia,” in contrast to Utopia.

Region

Americas: Latin America and Caribbean, North America
Asia: Eastern Asia, Southeastern Asia, Southern Asia
Europe: Central and Eastern Europe, Western Europe
Oceania: Australia and New Zealand
